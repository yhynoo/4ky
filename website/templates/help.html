1. Searching

The primary function of 4ky is searching for signs and sign combinations in the proto-cuneiform administrative corpus. There are two main modes: single query search and coordinated search.

Searching for a single query
To look for a single sign, type its transcription into the search bar, for example EN.
You can also search for a sign combination: then, you need to type all signs that the combination consists of, separated by spaces, like BU PAP.
The results you see are basic statistics and all a list of all the tablets where all elements of your query appear in a single case, regardless of the order of signs. You can then click on the title of the tablet to see it in CDLI.

Coordinated search
You can also look for tablets where multiple signs or sign combinations appear on the same tablet, but not necessarily in the same case. To do that, you need to enter your queries separated with a comma, for example: NAMESZDA, UKKIN or UDU, TE RAD SANGA.
The results will similar to those described above.

Filters and normalization
You can look for tablets only from specific periods or sites, or a combination of both. Additionally, you can choose to distinguish sign variants (eg. search specifically for DUG~a instead of all types of DUG and "dismantle" compound signs. Dismantling compound signs makes the search engine treat their elements as separate signs – eg. searching for SZE~a will find instances where it is inscribed into other signs as well, like |NINDA2xSZE~a|. Filters work the same for single and coordinated queries.

2. Transcriptions

The transcriptions used for browsing are sourced from CDLI. The following conventions are upheld:

SZ for shin
H for khet

Regular numbers are used instead of subscripts, so LU2 for LÚ and sign variants are written after the ~ sign, so SZE~a represents the a variant of the ŠE sign.

Additionally, a number of changes have been introduced:

Removed signs
[ ]
( ) other than in numerals
# for reconstructed signs
, after numerals
Editing signs
X replaced by ?
[...] replaced by ...
